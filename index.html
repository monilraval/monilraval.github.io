About Me

My Story

From India to Bavaria.

"I left everything familiar to build something real."

I grew up in Rajkot, India, with an engineering mindset. always curious, always asking why things work the way they do, and whether they could work better. After completing my BSc and then my MSc in Information Technology, I had a choice: stay comfortable, or go somewhere the problems are harder and the stakes are higher.

I chose Germany. Not for a job that was handed to me, for a fundamentally different way of building a career. A culture that values Präzision, Gründlichkeit, and long-term thinking. I arrived speaking limited German, knowing almost nobody, and with a clear hypothesis: that someone with technical depth, operational instincts, and a genuine hunger to deliver could build something meaningful here.

That hypothesis has been validated three times over. At Bosch, I learned what enterprise change management looks like at scale. At AGCO, I learned what it means to own a product that affects multiple countries and thousands of users. At Amazon, I learned what real operational pressure looks like — and how to lead people through it without losing the thread of performance.

In parallel, I'm building. ClarusHorizon.com is my live experiment in what happens when you apply product methodology and AI tooling to a problem you're willing to iterate on for years. ShiftSense AI is my answer to a problem I see every shift on the Amazon floor. I'm not waiting for someone to give me the interesting problems — I'm creating them.

My Philosophy

Why I build when I don't have to.

Most people in operations or product management roles talk about AI. They attend the webinars, read the newsletters, and include "AI-curious" in their LinkedIn bio. I build with it instead, because the gap between reading about a tool and actually using it to ship something real is where most people's understanding stops and mine begins.

Every project in this lab starts with a genuine problem I've personally experienced, not a portfolio-building exercise. ShiftSense AI came from a morning shift where I couldn't get reliable demand forecasts. PRD Generator came from my third consecutive hour writing boilerplate at AGCO.

The work is imperfect and iterating. That's the point. Real learning happens in the gap between the first commit and the version that actually works.
