My Story

From Surat to Bavaria.

"I chose Germany because the problems are harder and the stakes are higher."

I grew up in Surat, India, with an engineering mindset. After completing my BCA and then my MSc in Information Technology at GLS University, I had a deliberate choice to make: stay in a comfortable environment, or move to a country where precision, accountability, and long-term thinking are the minimum standard — not the aspiration.

I chose Germany. I arrived with limited German, no professional network, and a clear hypothesis: that someone with technical depth, a genuine interest in how organisations operate, and the discipline to keep improving could build something meaningful in the German market.

That hypothesis is being validated — slowly and honestly. At Bosch, I learned how enterprise change management works at scale. At AGCO, I owned a real product backlog for the first time and understood what it means to be accountable for a platform used across multiple countries. At Amazon, I'm experiencing how world-class operations actually function at the floor level — a perspective that most product managers never get.

In parallel, I'm building AI tools (ShiftSense AI, PRD Generator) and pushing my German from B1 toward B2. My career is 4 years old in Germany. The trajectory is deliberate and the destination is clear: Product Owner or Operations excellence roles where data, process, and stakeholder management intersect.
